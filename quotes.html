Buddhist Quotes from Various Traditions

Basic Buddhist Teachings

"Do not dwell in the past, do not dream of the future, concentrate the mind on the present moment."

- Buddha

"Hatred does not cease by hatred, but only by love; this is the eternal rule."

- Buddha (Dhammapada)

"Peace comes from within. Do not seek it without."

- Buddha

"The mind is everything. What you think you become."

- Buddha

"Three things cannot be long hidden: the sun, the moon, and the truth."

- Buddha

Theravada Buddhism

"As a water bead on a lotus leaf, as water on a red lily, does not adhere, so the sage does not adhere to the seen, the heard, or the sensed."

- Sutta Nipata

"The root of suffering is attachment."

- Buddha

"Be a lamp unto yourself."

- Buddha (Mahaparinibbana Sutta)

"Ardently do today what must be done. Who knows? Tomorrow, death comes."

- Bhaddekaratta Sutta

"To be idle is a short road to death and to be diligent is a way of life; foolish people are idle, wise people are diligent."

- Buddha

Mahayana Buddhism

"If you light a lamp for somebody, it will also brighten your path."

- Buddha

"Regard this fleeting world like this: Like stars fading and vanishing at dawn, like bubbles on a fast-moving stream, like morning dewdrops evaporating on blades of grass, like a candle flickering in a strong wind... echoes, mirages, and phantoms, hallucinations, and like a dream."

- Buddha (Diamond Sutra)

"All that we are is the result of what we have thought: it is founded on our thoughts and made up of our thoughts."

- Dhammapada

"To avoid all evil, to cultivate good, and to cleanse one's mind — this is the teaching of the Buddhas."

- Dhammapada

"Just as a mother would protect her only child with her life, even so let one cultivate a boundless love towards all beings."

- Karaniya Metta Sutta

Zen Buddhism

"If you understand, things are just as they are; if you do not understand, things are just as they are."

- Zen Proverb

"The obstacle is the path."

- Zen Proverb

"When you realize nothing is lacking, the whole world belongs to you."

- Lao Tzu

"Before enlightenment, chop wood, carry water. After enlightenment, chop wood, carry water."

- Zen Proverb

"The instant you speak about a thing, you miss the mark."

- Zen Proverb

Vajrayana Buddhism

"The nature of mind is the nature of everything."

- Padmasambhava

"If you want to know your past life, look into your present condition; if you want to know your future life, look at your present actions."

- Padmasambhava

"The true sign of a practitioner is that he or she is constantly applying the teachings to tame the mind."

- Dilgo Khyentse Rinpoche

"The everyday practice is simply to develop a complete acceptance and openness to all situations and emotions, and to all people, experiencing everything totally without mental reservations and blockages, so that one never withdraws or centralizes into oneself."

- Chögyam Trungpa Rinpoche

"The ultimate goal of Buddhism is not to create a system of beliefs, but to reach enlightenment."

- Dalai Lama

- Thich Nhat Hanh

"If you want to fly, give up everything that weighs you down."

- Buddha

"You are the sky. Everything else is just the weather."

"The only way to bring peace to the earth is to learn to make our own life peaceful."

- Buddha
