Anapanasati (Mindfulness of Breathing)

Tradition: Theravada

Instructions:

  1. Sit comfortably with your back straight. You can be on a cushion on the floor or in a chair.
  2. Close your eyes and take a few deep breaths to settle your body and mind.
  3. Bring your attention to your breath. Notice the sensation of the breath entering and leaving your nostrils.
  4. Don't try to control your breath; simply observe it as it is.
  5. When your mind wanders (and it will), gently bring your attention back to your breath without judgment.
  6. Continue this practice for the duration of the meditation.

Metta Bhavana (Loving-Kindness Meditation)

Tradition: Theravada/Mahayana

Instructions:

  1. Sit comfortably and take a few deep breaths to center yourself.
  2. Begin by generating feelings of loving-kindness towards yourself. Repeat silently:
    "May I be happy. May I be well. May I be safe. May I be peaceful and at ease."
  3. Next, bring to mind a beloved person and direct the same wishes to them.
  4. Then, think of a neutral person (someone you neither like nor dislike) and wish them well.
  5. Now, think of a difficult person in your life and try to extend loving-kindness to them.
  6. Finally, extend your loving-kindness to all beings everywhere:
    "May all beings be happy. May all beings be well. May all beings be safe. May all beings be peaceful and at ease."

Zazen (Seated Meditation)

Tradition: Zen

Instructions:

  1. Sit on a cushion in full or half lotus position, or in a chair with your feet flat on the floor.
  2. Keep your back straight, as if an invisible string were pulling you up from the crown of your head.
  3. Rest your hands in your lap in the cosmic mudra: left hand on top of right, palms up, thumbs touching to form an oval.
  4. Lower your gaze and keep your eyes half-open, focused on a point on the floor about 3 feet in front of you.
  5. Breathe naturally through your nose.
  6. Simply sit, allowing thoughts to come and go without engaging with them. If you get caught up in thought, gently return to your posture and breath.

Vipassana (Insight Meditation)

Tradition: Theravada

Instructions:

  1. Sit comfortably with your back straight.
  2. Close your eyes and take a few deep breaths to settle yourself.
  3. Begin by focusing on your breath, noticing the sensations of breathing in your abdomen or nostrils.
  4. As you become more settled, expand your awareness to include all sensations in your body.
  5. Notice sensations as they arise - heat, cold, pressure, tingling, etc. Don't judge them, simply observe.
  6. If thoughts or emotions arise, notice them too. Label them simply as "thinking" or "feeling" and return to observing sensations.
  7. Continue this practice of moment-to-moment awareness, developing insight into the changing nature of all phenomena.

Tonglen (Giving and Taking)

Tradition: Tibetan

Instructions:

  1. Sit comfortably and take a few deep breaths to center yourself.
  2. Visualize someone who is suffering (this could be yourself, a loved one, or even all beings).
  3. As you inhale, imagine you are taking in their suffering as a dark cloud.
  4. As you exhale, imagine sending them relief and happiness in the form of bright light.
  5. Continue this rhythm of breathing in suffering and breathing out relief.
  6. If it becomes overwhelming, return to normal breathing and then resume when ready.
  7. Gradually expand your practice to include more and more beings.
